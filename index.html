Natalie Hernandez

Student, Reporter, Big dreamer

Natalie Hernandez is a native New Yorker and recent grduate of Pace University. At Pace, Natalie recieved a degree in digital journalism and minor in politics. Hernandez has previously interned at CBS in their Broadcast Publicity Department and CNBC in their Long Form Programming Unit. At CNBC Hernandez researched, shot content, and found interview subjects for CNBC Documentatries. You can find her name in the credits of the documentaties, "DNA TESTING: The Promise and Perils," "Fugitive CEO: The Carlos Ghosn Story," and "Supermarket Shock: Crisis in America's Food Supply."

Currently, Hernandez is a graduate student at the Craig Newmark Graduate School of Journalism. She hopes to become a broadcast journalist with a memorable presence.

About Natalie

Hernandez is a part of the Young Producers Club at the Apollo Theater. There, she works on aiding in the production of arts and education programs for teens at the Apollo. He has served as a host and correspondent for various Apollo Theater events.

Hernandez is a former peer educator and confidential resource for Pace F.I.R.E - Fighting Ignorance and Rape through Education.At Pace University she served as the Chair of Social Unity and Justice on student government and president of a womens empowerment group. In 2019, Hernandez was awarded with the Jefferson Award, a public service award that recognizes individuals for their public and volunteer service, and dedication to improving the quality of life in their communities.

Hernandez is a former host and wither for Manhattan Neighorhood Network's Girls Empowered. While at Girls Empowered Hernandez helped to develop a show that aimed to create intersectional conversations about experiences affecting women of color and marginalized groups.
